Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)

On September 27, 2024, a delegation of professors from Zimbabwe visited our department to renew their Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the International Relations Office. Their visit reflected a strong interest in advancing collaboration, particularly in the area of cybersecurity specialization.

During their visit, Dr. Varun Kumar provided a comprehensive overview of the cutting-edge tools and technologies available in the department’s cybersecurity specialized laboratories, showcasing the institution’s capabilities and commitment to excellence in this field.

Faculty Advisor: Dr.G.Suseela

Thanks to the mentors, I was able to use Machine Learning to tackle real world problems in the field of Cardiology. One of the highlights of the internship was to have weekly meet-ups where we had to present research papers to our instructors. This widened my perspective in the domain and polished my critical thinking skills. The research work further helped me develop my coding and problem-solving skills. Furthermore, I received hands-on experience in applications such as app development and embedded systems as well as signal processing. It took a little time to absorb everything but once I did, the experience was life changing for me.
