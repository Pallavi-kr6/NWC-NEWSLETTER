Internet of Things Specialization

Specialization Summary

The Internet of Things (IoT) Specialization at SRM Institute of Science and Technology (SRM IST) under the SRG focuses on advanced IoT concepts, applications, and security. It covers sensor networks, edge computing, AI-driven IoT solutions, and blockchain-based security. The curriculum integrates hands-on projects, industry collaborations, and real-world problem-solving. Research areas include smart cities, healthcare, industrial automation, and environmental monitoring. The program emphasizes AI-empowered IoT, energy-efficient protocols, and cybersecurity measures. Faculty and students actively engage in funded research, patents, and publications. Industry partnerships foster internships, certifications, and career opportunities. This specialization equips students with cutting-edge skills to drive innovation in the IoT ecosystem.

Specialization Labs:

  • Intel Unnati IoT Lab
  • Things of Future Lab
  • SRM Latrobe Joint Centre of Eminence Next Generation Lab

Purpose of the Specialized Lab

Intel Unnati IoT Lab

SRMIST, in collaboration with Intel Technologies Pvt. Ltd., Bangalore, proudly operates the Intel Unnati IoT Solutions Lab. This lab is equipped with state-of-the-art edge computing kits, AI-based foundation kits, and Robomaker kits to empower students and researchers in their pursuit of innovation. Our mission is to integrate AI with IoT technologies to create transformative solutions, bridging the gap between academics and industry. Through internships and consultancy projects mentored by industry experts, we strive to equip young engineers with industry-ready skills

Project Spotlight

Arc to Accuracy

An AI-powered website hosted on the Intel Unnati Lab server, “Arc to Accuracy,” was launched at the 2nd International Conference on Innovations in Physical Education, Sports & Yogic Sciences on 9th November 2024. Developed in collaboration with SRMIST’s Directorate of Sports, this project analyzes basketball players’ performance, assisting coaches in refining training strategies.

Field Trip Experience

A field trip was organized on 4th October 2024 to SRMIST Agricultural Sciences, Acharapakkam, for AIoT project data collection and validation. Students gathered pig health parameters for a monitoring system. Weed images were collected for a weed detection and cutting project. This hands-on experience strengthened students’ understanding of real-time AIoT applications

Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)

The Internet of Things (IoT) specialization MoUs are Swinburne University of Technology, Sarawak Campus (2024) and Intel Technology Pvt. Ltd. (2024). The Swinburne University MoU, lasting three years, focuses on mutual collaboration in research projects, publications, and student, faculty exchange programs. Similarly, the Intel Technology MoU, also for three years, provides opportunities for IoT-related courses and industrial training at Intel. These collaborations aim to enhance academic research, skill development, and industry exposure, ensuring students gain practical expertise and career opportunities in the growing field of IoT.
