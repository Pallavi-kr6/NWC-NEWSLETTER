The construction of the metaverse faces multiple substantial obstacles despite its promising prospects. The ability to scale remains a vital challenge because supporting millions of users in real-time demands sophisticated resource optimization and distributed computing systems. User identity protection together with data ownership management and unauthorized access prevention represents essential measures to resolve privacy and security issues. Widespread adoption of VR and AR devices requires improvements in their affordability alongside better comfort features and extended battery life. The establishment of worldwide standards for platform integration and communication remains an active challenge. The metaverse enables transformative applications which span multiple domains. Virtual classrooms and simulation-based environments within education and training deliver powerful and successful learning opportunities. The entertainment sector uses immersive gaming experiences together with virtual concerts and sports events to benefit its operations. The combination of virtual stores and showrooms in e-commerce delivers personalized shopping experiences to customers while virtual offices and 3D meeting spaces enable real-time collaboration between global teams. The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) launched MetaHKUST which stands as the first physical-digital twin campuses in the metaverse worldwide. The mixed virtual reality platform provides an immersive educational experience that lets students from different locations join classes together as if they shared the same physical classroom. The metaverse’s future development will integrate advanced AI systems for personalized interactions and quantum computing technology for enhanced security features and brain-computer interfaces for direct digital system user communication.
